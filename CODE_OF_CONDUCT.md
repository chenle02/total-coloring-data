# Code of conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to a respectful, rigorous, and welcoming research and
engineering community. Participation is open regardless of experience,
background, identity, disability, nationality, career stage, or viewpoint.
Scientific disagreement is expected; personal hostility is not.

## Expected behavior

- Discuss claims, evidence, code, and reproducibility with care and specificity.
- Separate correction of a result from judgment of the person who produced it.
- Give credit, disclose relevant conflicts, and respect embargoed or private
  research material.
- Welcome questions and acknowledge uncertainty or mistakes promptly.
- Respect maintainers' review, security, and data-governance procedures.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, discrimination, threats, stalking, sexualized attention, deliberate
intimidation, doxxing, publication of private communications, plagiarism,
retaliation, and sustained disruptive conduct are unacceptable. Knowingly
misrepresenting computational evidence or pressuring others to suppress a
good-faith correction also violates this policy.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository discussions, reviews, issues, project
meetings, and public spaces where someone is representing this project.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to `chenle02@gmail.com` with the subject
`[total-coloring conduct]`. Include enough context for a fair review, but do not
send unnecessary sensitive information. Reports will be handled as privately
as practicable; information may be shared with additional neutral reviewers
only when needed to assess or resolve the concern.

Maintainers may remove content, issue a warning, limit participation, impose a
temporary or permanent ban, or refer credible threats or unlawful behavior to
the appropriate institution or authority. Anyone directly involved in a report
must recuse themselves from deciding its outcome when a neutral reviewer is
available. Retaliation against a reporter or participant in a review is
prohibited.

Questions about this policy may be sent to the same contact address.
